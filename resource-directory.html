How AI could maintain the directory

  1. Find candidate resources from official websites, 211-style listings, school pages, state agencies, and local nonprofits.
  2. Detect duplicates by comparing names, phone numbers, addresses, service areas, and website domains.
  3. Summarize services into plain-language descriptions with eligibility notes and confidence levels.
  4. Check broken links and flag redirects, timeouts, or pages that no longer mention the listed service.
  5. Flag outdated listings when verification dates get old, hours change, or source pages differ from stored data.
  6. Send verification reminders to assigned staff or partner agencies before high-risk information expires.
  7. Use a review queue so suggested additions and changes are approved by a human before publication.
